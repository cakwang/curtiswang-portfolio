I'm a Learning Experience Designer thriving at the intersection of education, design, and technology.

tl;dr

My mission is to preserve the humanity of learning by combining cross-disciplinary approaches with evidence-based research to build rigorous, accessible experiences. Currently at Kaiser Permanente, I design scalable training and change management solutions while exploring the ethical integration of AI into modern work culture. My academic background spans degrees from the University of Toronto, Stanford, and USC, culminating in doctoral research on the existential challenges of modern instructional design. To bridge the gap between traditional models and contemporary digital realities, I leverage insights from diverse disciplines from game design to UI/UX to behavioral economics.

Through both professional practice and research, my lifelong mission is to help preserve the humanity of deep learning amidst rapid and often disruptive change. Over time, this has taken various forms across diverse contexts, from K-12 teaching to ed-tech integration, and more recently, to higher education, informal learning, and larger-scale corporate change management. Across virtually all these roles, I've seen that traditional learning models alone are insufficient, and have increasingly found it necessary to combine cross-disciplinary approaches with evidence-based research to build rigorous learning experiences that simultaneously broaden access and deepen learning outcomes. More recently, I've also become more involved in organizational capacity-building efforts leading to an even greater emphasis on how I foster creative confidence and growth mindset within design teams.

Currently at Kaiser Permanente, much of my work involves consulting and producing content for large-scale organizational change management and brand awareness initiatives. Designing for clinical staff, business professionals, and our patient community alike, I also build scalable training solutions and am exploring the ethical integration of artificial intelligence into the current culture of work. Prior to KP, I worked in higher education--both in formal institutions where I was involved in systematic program development and process improvement, and in agencies working with diverse clients to design bespoke digital learning experiences.

I received an H.B.Sc. in Communication Theory and Human Biology, followed by a Master of Teaching degree and K-12 teacher certification from the University of Toronto. With a deeper focus on technologically-mediated learning, I then earned my M.A. in Learning Design & Technology from Stanford University. Concurrently while working in the field, I completed my Ed.D. in Educational Leadership at the University of Southern California. My doctoral research investigated the pragmatic and existential challenges of modern instructional design practice in corporate settings.

I often say that I want learners to spend less time on the mechanics of learning and more on the substance of learning. In a landscape where online learning has become a staple among adult learners, the rigid "grammar" of traditional instructional design is often at odds with the behaviors and motivations of navigating today's online spaces. The ADDIE model still offers some usefulness in organizing high-level design priorities, but is impractical in isolation. To bridge this gap, my approach is to leverage insights from diverse domains, from game design and UI/UX to digital marketing and behavioral economics, to produce experiences that are authentic and relevant. At the same time, modern technologies like big data and artificial intelligence have enabled flexible workflows that meet contemporary digital realities.

Outside of my work in education, I make music as a mainstage EDM artist and as a hip-hop producer for other artists. Even then, I often like to find opportunities to share educational content! I'm also a competitive saber fencer and coach, and I play a ton of Dance Dance Revolution.
